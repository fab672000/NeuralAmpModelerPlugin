6 ** File: connection.h - JNL TCP connection interface
7 ** License: see jnetlib.h
8 **
9 ** Usage:
10 ** 1. Create a JNL_Connection object, optionally specifying a JNL_IAsyncDNS
11 ** object to use (or NULL for none, or JNL_CONNECTION_AUTODNS for auto),
12 ** and the send and receive buffer sizes.
13 ** 2. Call connect() to have it connect to a host/port (the hostname will be
14 ** resolved if possible).
15 ** 3. call run() with the maximum send/recv amounts, and optionally parameters
16 ** so you can tell how much has been send/received. You want to do this a lot, while:
17 ** 4. check get_state() to check the state of the connection. The states are:
18 ** JNL_Connection::STATE_ERROR
19 ** - an error has occured on the connection. the connection has closed,
20 ** and you can no longer write to the socket (there still might be
21 ** data in the receive buffer - use recv_bytes_available()).
22 ** JNL_Connection::STATE_NOCONNECTION
23 ** - no connection has been made yet. call connect() already! :)
24 ** JNL_Connection::STATE_RESOLVING
25 ** - the connection is still waiting for a JNL_AsycnDNS to resolve the
26 ** host.
27 ** JNL_Connection::STATE_CONNECTING
28 ** - the asynchronous call to connect() is still running.
29 ** JNL_Connection::STATE_CONNECTED
30 ** - the connection has connected, all is well.
31 ** JNL_Connection::STATE_CLOSING
32 ** - the connection is closing. This happens after a call to close,
33 ** without the quick parameter set. This means that the connection
34 ** will close once the data in the send buffer is sent (data could
35 ** still be being received when it would be closed). After it is
36 ** closed, the state will transition to:
37 ** JNL_Connection::STATE_CLOSED
38 ** - the connection has closed, generally without error. There still
39 ** might be data in the receieve buffer, use recv_bytes_available().
40 ** 5. Use send() and send_string() to send data. You can use
41 ** send_bytes_in_queue() to see how much has yet to go out, or
42 ** send_bytes_available() to see how much you can write. If you use send()
43 ** or send_string() and not enough room is available, both functions will
44 ** return error ( < 0)
45 ** 6. Use recv() and recv_line() to get data. If you want to see how much data
46 ** there is, use recv_bytes_available() and recv_lines_available(). If you
47 ** call recv() and not enough data is available, recv() will return how much
48 ** data was actually read. See comments at the function defs.
49 **
50 ** 7. To close, call close(1) for a quick close, or close() for a close that will
51 ** make the socket close after sending all the data sent.
52 **
53 ** 8. delete ye' ol' object.
54 */

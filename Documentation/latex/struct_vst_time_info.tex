\begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
\mbox{\hyperlink{struct_vst_time_info_ad46fad394f437e1b283f2e36285be146}{Vst\+Time\+Info\+::sample\+Pos}} \+:Current Position. It must always be valid, and should not cost a lot to ask for. The sample position is ahead of the time displayed to the user. In sequencer stop mode, its value does not change. A 32 bit integer is too small for sample positions, and it\textquotesingle{}s a double to make it easier to convert between ppq and samples. 

\mbox{\hyperlink{struct_vst_time_info_aa243da6be1161a971983e68be39e7a28}{Vst\+Time\+Info\+::ppq\+Pos}} \+: At tempo 120, 1 quarter makes 1/2 second, so 2.\+0 ppq translates to 48000 samples at 48k\+Hz sample rate. .25 ppq is one sixteenth note then. if you need something like 480ppq, you simply multiply ppq by that scaler. 

\mbox{\hyperlink{struct_vst_time_info_a52d38a95a4c34ea3fb060388d4b412d2}{Vst\+Time\+Info\+::bar\+Start\+Pos}} \+: Say we\textquotesingle{}re at bars/beats readout 3.\+3.\+3. That\textquotesingle{}s 2 bars + 2 q + 2 sixteenth, makes 2 $\ast$ 4 + 2 + .25 = 10.\+25 ppq. at tempo 120, that\textquotesingle{}s 10.\+25 $\ast$ .5 = 5.\+125 seconds, times 48000 = 246000 samples (if my calculator servers me well \+:-\/). 

\mbox{\hyperlink{struct_vst_time_info_aa64d2f92ed1a617aae11b3c785073ad7}{Vst\+Time\+Info\+::samples\+To\+Next\+Clock}} \+: M\+I\+DI Clock Resolution (24 per Quarter Note), can be negative the distance to the next midi clock (24 ppq, pulses per quarter) in samples. unless sample\+Pos falls precicely on a midi clock, this will either be negative such that the previous M\+I\+DI clock is addressed, or positive when referencing the following (future) M\+I\+DI clock. 
\end{DoxyNote}

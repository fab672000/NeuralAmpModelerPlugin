\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
\mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} contain geometry. \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} may be empty, or contain one or more verbs that outline a figure. \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} always starts with a move verb to a Cartesian coordinate, and may be followed by additional verbs that add lines or curves. Adding a close verb makes the geometry into a continuous loop, a closed contour. \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} may contain any number of contours, each beginning with a move verb.

\mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} contours may contain only a move verb, or may also contain lines, quadratic beziers, conics, and cubic beziers. \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} contours may be open or closed.

When used to draw a filled area, \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} describes whether the fill is inside or outside the geometry. \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} also describes the winding rule used to fill overlapping contours.

Internally, \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} lazily computes metrics likes bounds and convexity. Call \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path_a6de7de42175fe3ce5ff92f3d2267ec2d}{Sk\+Path\+::update\+Bounds\+Cache}} to make \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} thread safe.

Approximates conic with quad array. Conic is constructed from start \mbox{\hyperlink{struct_sk_point}{Sk\+Point}} p0, control \mbox{\hyperlink{struct_sk_point}{Sk\+Point}} p1, end \mbox{\hyperlink{struct_sk_point}{Sk\+Point}} p2, and weight w. Quad array is stored in pts; this storage is supplied by caller. Maximum quad count is 2 to the pow2. Every third point in array shares last \mbox{\hyperlink{struct_sk_point}{Sk\+Point}} of previous quad and first \mbox{\hyperlink{struct_sk_point}{Sk\+Point}} of next quad. Maximum pts storage size is given by\+: (1 + 2 $\ast$ (1 $<$$<$ pow2)) $\ast$ sizeof(\+Sk\+Point).

conic weight determines the amount of influence conic control point has on the curve. w less than one represents an elliptical section. w greater than one represents a hyperbolic section. w equal to one represents a parabolic section.

Two quad curves are sufficient to approximate an elliptical conic with a sweep of up to 90 degrees; in this case, set pow2 to one.

Specifies whether \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} is volatile; whether it will be altered or discarded by the caller after it is drawn. \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} by default have volatile set false, allowing \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_base_device}{Sk\+Base\+Device}} to attach a cache of data which speeds repeated drawing.

Mark temporary paths, discarded or modified after use, as volatile to inform \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_base_device}{Sk\+Base\+Device}} that the path need not be cached.

Mark animating \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} volatile to improve performance. Mark unchanging \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} non-\/volatile to improve repeated rendering.

raster surface \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} draws are affected by volatile for some shadows. G\+PU surface \mbox{\hyperlink{class_sk_path}{Sk\+Path}} draws are affected by volatile for some shadows and concave geometries.
